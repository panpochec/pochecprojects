Schiff bases: data analysis and visualisation

Second part of the project consisted of analyzing and visualizing data from Part 1.

Having all of the data in one place allowed the multi-step analysis, consisting of building subsets of data, transforming and pivoting subsets and statistical analysis. Unfortunatelly, I can't reveal all of the data or even siginificant parts of the figures, because these studies are still waiting for publication. It should be noted, that the code used in this leg of work is much cleaner and sofisticated then the one used in the previous section, as develpment wasn't as rushed.

Part of the dataset from Part 1, in csv form.

Data from step one was loaded to the script in pd.DataFrame form.

Then, it was subseted for different kind of information, allowing summary statistics for all of the different substitution patterns to be constructed. In this step pivot tables were also build, in preparation for the visualization steps to be performed.

As the tables were pivoted, it was imperative to fill the data. At this stage it was neccessary to trasform from A-B naming scheme to Proximal-Distal (as relation to the scanned bridge). The studied compounds were symmetrical and that means if ring B is substituted by Br on position 3 it was scanned on two bridges - A and B. This gives information about energy profile of proximal Br - 3 substitution and distal Br - 3 substitution. Similarly, with ring A substituted in Br - 3 pattern and ring B in NO2 - 1 pattern, that means we have data for "Proximal Br - 3, Distal NO2 - 1" substitution, but also for "Proximal NO2 - 1, Distal Br - 3" substitution.

For tables containing different sets of data basic statistical analysis was performed - mean, standard deviation, maximal and minimal values for proximal and distal effect was recorded for parameters such as activation energy, second energy minimum, charges and more.

Part of the typical table containing statystycial analysis for a single parameter
It contains statistical data and the descriptor for Proximal-Distal effect.

The final section of the analysis was ploting heatmaps and preparing charge distribution schemes.

Heatmaps were prepared using Seaborn package upon the basis of pivot tables with proximal substitution as index and distal substitution as columns. Multiple different heatmaps were prepared to present key parameters that may have direct effect on bridge activation energy and second minimum.

Heatmaps were then connected with charge distribution schemes for proximal substitution, for which data were acquired using single-point Hirschfeld charges population distribution. Schemes were prepared using Pillow package, with sizes and colors of the circles corresponding to the charge difference from non-substituted entity.

The in-depth data analysis with conclusions will be fully available when material from those studies will be published. But considering everything described, this line of research has proven not only to yield sigificant results to date, but hase a great potential for future expansion.
